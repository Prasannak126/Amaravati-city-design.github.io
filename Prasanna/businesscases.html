Business Cases for Infrastructure (Mapping to SDG 9.1 and 9.4)

Targets

  • Develop resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and foster innovation.
  • Upgrade infrastructure and retrofit industries to make them sustainable, with increased resource-use efficiency and greater adoption of clean technologies and industrial processes.

Indicators

  • Proportion of the population with access to reliable infrastructure services (e.g., electricity, transportation).
  • Adoption of technologies aimed at improving resource efficiency and reducing emissions in infrastructure sectors.

Benefits

Social Benefits:

  • Community Connectivity: Improved infrastructure like roads, bridges, and communication networks can connect remote communities, reducing isolation and increasing access to essential services.
  • Enhanced Quality of Life: Better infrastructure can provide communities with safer, more reliable access to water, electricity, transportation, and healthcare, improving living standards.
  • Job Creation: The development and maintenance of infrastructure generate employment opportunities in both construction and operational sectors, benefiting local communities.

Economic Benefits:

  • Economic Growth: Infrastructure investment is closely linked to economic growth, as it enhances productivity, reduces transaction costs, and facilitates trade.
  • Industry Boost: Improved infrastructure supports industries by providing reliable transportation, electricity, and digital connectivity, which reduces operational costs and enhances competitiveness.
  • Investment Attraction: Quality infrastructure attracts both domestic and international investment by providing a stable environment for business operations.

Environmental Benefits:

  • Sustainable Infrastructure: Developing infrastructure with sustainability in mind (e.g., green buildings, renewable energy installations) can reduce long-term environmental impacts.
  • Resource Efficiency: Using advanced technologies to monitor and optimize infrastructure usage (such as smart grids or water management systems) can reduce waste and enhance efficiency.
  • Climate Resilience: Climate-adaptive infrastructure ensures communities and industries are better prepared for extreme weather events and environmental changes.

Refining Business Cases on SDGs

Smart Cities

Objective: Promote the development of smart cities that leverage technology to improve urban services such as transportation, waste management, water distribution, and energy usage.

Solution: Use advanced sensors, data analytics, and machine learning algorithms to optimize resource distribution, reduce energy consumption, and ensure sustainability.

Example: Implement smart traffic management systems to reduce congestion and energy use, and apply AI for optimizing waste collection routes to reduce fuel consumption.

Green Infrastructure

Objective: Develop green infrastructure such as parks, green roofs, and permeable pavements that can absorb rainwater, reduce urban heat islands, and enhance biodiversity.

Solution: Design and build infrastructure projects that incorporate natural elements to reduce environmental impact while promoting ecological sustainability.

Example: Implement green roofing on buildings to manage stormwater and reduce air conditioning needs, or create urban forests to provide recreational spaces and improve air quality.

Sustainable Transport Networks

Objective: Design transport systems that minimize energy consumption, reduce emissions, and promote sustainable mobility, such as electric vehicles (EVs) and mass transit.

Solution: Invest in EV charging infrastructure, and expand public transportation networks to reduce traffic congestion and air pollution.

Example: Develop bicycle-sharing systems, electric buses, and pedestrian-friendly walkways to encourage environmentally friendly transport choices.
